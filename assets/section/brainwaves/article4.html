Chinese grad student kidnapped believed dead
Kevin Calderon

The ongoing investigation of Chinese grad student Yingying Zhang’s disappearance
continues as the suspected kidnapper, Brendt Christensen, has been arrested and
taken in for questioning by the FBI..

According to authorities, the FBI no longer believe Zhang is alive based on a confession
by the suspect while he was under surveillance last Thursday, in which Christensen
described how he kidnapped Zhang.

Christensen is a grad student of the University of Illinois, the same school that Zhang
was last seen at. According to a UI Professor, Christensen had no previous educational
or behavioral issues and had been a teaching assistant in the physics department until
this past May.


During earlier stages of the investigation, FBI agents and police officers obtained a
warrant to search Christensen’s vehicle, which matched the one shown in street
security footage. At the same time, the authorities interviewed Christensen at the
bureau’s Champaign office.
While conducting the interview, the suspect admitted to picking Zhang up at the place
shown in the security footage, but claimed to have dropped her after driving a few
blocks. Another occupant of Christensen’s residence consented to a search and seizure
of the residence, during which the investigators found the suspect’s phone was
collected.
Upon searching the cellphone, investigators found that Christensen had visited a fetish
website with articles titled “Abduction 101” and “planning a kidnapping.”
Christensen made his first appearance at the federal courthouse in Urbana, Illinois on
Monday the 3rd. During his entire nine-minute appearance, the accused remained
nearly silent and did not enter any plea.

Students and professors at UI, as well as sympathetic strangers, held a rally for
Yingying Zhang’s family. Oddly enough, Brendt Christensen attended the rally for his
victim, as shown in a photo taken from CNN.
The day following the rally, Christensen, who had already been under surveillance for
several weeks, was arrested.
Christensen now faces a federal kidnapping charge, and, if convicted, could be
sentenced to life in prison. According to the suspect’s lawyer, Christensen has no
criminal history and it is unknown whether he will declare his innocence or not.
There are conflicting opinions from China, particularly the many students from the
country who travel to the United States to attend school. While many are concerned
about Zhang’s disappearance, many others are still unshaken, citing China’s own high
rate of violent crimes.
Members of Zhang’s family who had travelled to the States to assist in the search for
her have been informed of Christensen’s arrest.
